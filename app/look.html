Diving in Indonesia
Indonesia, which occupies a vast area between the continental parts of Southeast Asia and Australia, is made up of more than 17,500 islands. Home to 25% of the world's fish and 15% of all coral reefs, this beautiful country is a must-see for every passionate diver.

The choice of diving regions in Indonesia is also impressive - so much so that divers of all levels may even feel somewhat spoiled. Popular regions of Indonesia include Bali, Gili Islands, Lombok, North Sulawesi, Komodo, Wakatobi, Lembeh, Manado, Raja Ampat and others. However, among the thousands of local islands, you will find many other incredible places.

Volcano Tours in Indonesia
Indonesia is located in the most seismically active zone of the Earth - there are 400 volcanoes on its territory, 80 of which are active. Traveling on the volcanoes of Indonesia is a dream! Join our tour and you will climb the Ijen volcano with its turquoise crater lake, meet the sunrise on Bromo, watch the eruption of Merapi volcano and swim in the hot springs at the foot of Batur. All these treasures are located on 2 amazing islands of the country: Bali and Java.

Starting in Yogyakarta and ending on the coast of Bali, our exciting tour will embrace both the cultural and natural diversity of Indonesia. You will see cultural monuments of Hinduism and Buddhism, visit the jungle and volcanic valleys.

Fauna observation
Indonesia has the richest fauna of any country in the world. Almost all major classes of animals living in Indonesia are diverse. At the beginning of the XXI century, 515 species of mammals, 1531 species of birds, 122 species of butterflies, more than 600 species of reptiles and more than 270 species of amphibians were registered here. At the same time, 39% of mammals and 36% of birds are endemic. Among the most famous endemics are the Komodo dragon, Kulya's deer, babirussa, and Tonka macaque.

Many animals are under threat, and some species are declining at a very rapid rate. So, only out of mammals 140 species are classified by the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) as threatened, 15 of them are considered to be on the verge of extinction. Among the latter are such animals as the orangutan, the Javanese rhinoceros, the Sumatran tiger.
